Porblem solving

A story of when i was stuck.

I was in HTML and CSS trying to make one of my texts green, so i decided to use a class system, and assign it the class of green. It should've been simple and easy, but it wasn't, it quickly became a big issue as i couldn't progress further in my challenge, i started to panic as my text was turning purple instead of my chosen color of green.

I started to calm myself, i needed to focus, i thought back to when i was taught problem solving techniques, but i didnt actually know many at the time so i just read my code over and over again, tried diffrent ways of writing the code, tried to use ID's instead of classes, but it was futile, it seemed there was nothing that could be done to turn my text green.

And just as i was getting comfortable with the color purple i realised something after reading my code again, there it is, i found the problem. I guess whoever made HTML and CSS were american because i have been spelling the word color all wrong. I had been spelling it a true normal person with a U colour. I changed the spelling and sure enough it worked like a charm, i had finnally achieved the color (colour) green.

To this day i still dont know why a U in colour made the text turn purple, but i learned from it and have decided maybe Americans can spell properly, so i shall spell color as colour again.

But really what i learnt most of all is that spelling is critical, a small mistake like that had such a big impact on what i was doing, so know whenever something goes wrong i always check my spelling first.

Trying something is always good, i just try something even if im pretty sure it wont work but sometimes it does!

I have never tried the rubber ducky method but i have heard about it, it's where you get a little rubber ducky or something similiar and you talk to it and tell it what youre doing, and hopefully you will notice something as you say it aloud.

Console.logging is helpful, although i havent really used it all that much yet.
